## Problem
Diffusion-based real-world image super-resolution (Real-ISR) delivers strong perceptual quality but requires slow multi-step inference; existing one-step distillation methods inherit artifacts from weak teacher SR models and rely on real LR-HR paired data. FluxSR targets one-step Real-ISR built on a large flow-matching text-to-image model (FLUX.1-dev) without needing a pretrained teacher ISR model.

## Method
FluxSR uses FLUX.1-dev (a ~12B-parameter flow-matching DiT) as both base and teacher, and proposes Flow Trajectory Distillation (FTD) to collapse the multi-step flow into a single step. Instead of fine-tuning the model directly on SR, FTD keeps the original noise-to-image T2I flow fixed and derives the LR-to-HR flow trajectory by combining two pre-computed flows — the noise-to-image flow (from FLUX) and a separately trained noise-to-LR flow — yielding a "noise-free flow" relation that eliminates the need for a teacher ISR model and for real paired data. Training uses synthetic noise-image pairs generated by FLUX with RealESRGAN-style degradation. To suppress high-frequency artifacts and boost realism, it adds TV-LPIPS (LPIPS on images plus LPIPS on their total-variation/edge maps) and an Attention Diversification Loss (ADL) that reduces token similarity (cosine similarity to the mean token feature) in the transformer to diversify features.

## Key Contributions
- Flow Trajectory Distillation (FTD): distills a multi-step flow-matching model into a one-step Real-ISR model by learning the SR flow trajectory conditioned on a frozen T2I flow, without a pretrained teacher ISR model
- Noise-free flow construction that derives the LR-to-HR flow from a noise-to-image flow and a noise-to-LR flow, removing dependence on real LR-HR paired datasets (trains on ~2,400 synthetic 1024x1024 noise-image pairs from FLUX + RealESRGAN degradation)
- TV-LPIPS perceptual loss combining standard LPIPS with LPIPS on total-variation maps to target high-frequency artifacts
- Attention Diversification Loss (ADL) regularizer reducing token similarity in transformer layers to eliminate high-frequency artifacts
- First to leverage a large flow-matching T2I model (FLUX.1-dev) for one-step Real-ISR

## Results
4x Real-ISR on RealSR, RealSet65, and DIV2K-val. Reports best no-reference metrics among one-step methods on all test sets (e.g., RealSR MUSIQ ~68.95, Q-Align ~4.38) while trading off fidelity metrics (PSNR ~24.83 on RealSR; ablation FTD config: PSNR 24.67 / MUSIQ 67.84 / MANIQA 0.5203 / Q-Align 4.1473). Claims it outperforms existing one-step diffusion Real-ISR methods (OSEDiff, SinSR, ResShift) and surpasses multi-step StableSR across datasets. Single inference step; ~12B-parameter FLUX.1-dev backbone. Note: exact full result tables not all confirmed from accessible text.

## Significance
FluxSR shows that a billion-parameter flow-matching T2I backbone can be distilled to a single inference step for Real-ISR while avoiding both a teacher SR model and real paired training data, advancing one-step generative SR by exploiting flow trajectories rather than conventional score/consistency distillation.
